BUDGETING (Ch 9)
Production = Sales + Ending Inv - Beginning Inv
Materials Purchases = Production Needs + Ending Mat - Beginning Mat
Materials Needed = Units to Produce × Mat per Unit
Labor Cost = Units to Produce × Hours/Unit × Rate/Hour
Cash = Beginning + Receipts - Disbursements
Flow: Sales → Production → Materials/Labor/OH → Cash → Statements
VARIANCES (Ch 10)
Materials:
Price Var = AQ × (AP - SP)
Quantity Var = SP × (AQ - SQ)
SQ = Units PRODUCED × Std per Unit
Labor:
Rate Var = AH × (AR - SR)
Efficiency Var = SR × (AH - SH)
Variable OH:
Spending Var = Actual - (AH × VOHR)
Efficiency Var = VOHR × (AH - SH)
Fixed OH:
Budget Var = Actual - Budgeted
Volume Var = Budgeted - Applied
F = Actual < Std (cost less), U = Actual > Std (cost more)
DECISION MAKING (Ch 12)
Relevant = Future + Different + Avoidable
Sunk = Always Irrelevant
Make-or-Buy:
Compare: Make (Var + Avoidable Fixed) vs Buy (Price)
Special Order:
Accept if: Price > Variable Costs (if capacity available)
Product Mix:
Rank by: CM per Constrained Resource
CM per Hour = CM per Unit ÷ Hours per Unit
Keep or Drop:
Compare: Segment Margin vs Avoidable Costs Saved
ROI & RESIDUAL INCOME (Ch 13)
ROI = Net Operating Income ÷ Avg Operating Assets
ROI = Margin × Turnover
Margin = NOI ÷ Sales
Turnover = Sales ÷ Avg Assets
RI = NOI - (Assets × Required ROI)
ROI: % return, may discourage investments
RI: $ above required, encourages all profitable investments
Segment Margin:
Revenue - Variable Costs - Traceable Fixed
BALANCED SCORECARD (Ch 11)
Four Perspectives:
1. Financial: ROI, Profit, Revenue Growth
2. Customer: Satisfaction, Market Share, Retention
3. Internal Processes: Quality, Cycle Time, Efficiency
4. Learning & Growth: Training, Employee Satisfaction
Chain:
Learning → Processes → Customer → Financial
Financial = Lagging, Non-financial = Leading
CVP ANALYSIS (Ch 3)
CM per Unit = Price - Variable Cost
CM Ratio = CM ÷ Sales
Break-Even Units = Fixed Costs ÷ CM per Unit
Break-Even $ = Fixed Costs ÷ CM Ratio
Target Profit Units = (Fixed + Target) ÷ CM per Unit
Margin of Safety = Actual - Break-Even
Operating Leverage = CM ÷ NOI
% Change Profit = Leverage × % Change Sales
COST CLASSIFICATION (Ch 1-2)
Function:Product (DM+DL+OH) vs Period (S&A)
Behavior:Variable (total changes) vs Fixed (total constant)
Traceability:Direct (traceable) vs Indirect (allocated)
Relevance:Relevant (differs) vs Irrelevant (same/sunk)
Product Costs: Inventory → COGS (when sold)
Period Costs: Expense immediately
COSTING SYSTEMS
Job Order (Ch 4-5):
POHR = Est Overhead ÷ Est Base
Applied = POHR × Actual Base
Job Cost = DM + DL + Applied OH
Variance = Actual - Applied
Process (Ch 6):
EU = Physical Units × % Complete
Cost per EU = Total Cost ÷ EU
Materials EU: Check when added (beginning/end/evenly)
ABC (Ch 7):
Activity Rate = Cost Pool ÷ Total Driver
Product Cost = Σ(Rate × Product Usage)
QUICK REFERENCE:
• Variable: Total↑ with activity, Per unit constant
• Fixed: Total constant, Per unit↓ with activity
• Direct/Indirect ≠ Variable/Fixed (independent!)
• Relevant: Will it change? Yes = Relevant
• Sunk: Can't change = Always ignore
• Product Costs: Inventory → COGS (when sold)
• Period Costs: Expense immediately
• F Variance: Actual < Std (cost less)
• U Variance: Actual > Std (cost more)
Key Formulas: CM = Price - VC | BE = FC ÷ CM | Target = (FC + Profit) ÷ CM | ROI = NOI ÷ Assets = Margin × Turnover | RI = NOI - (Assets × Req ROI)
