⭐ VARIABLE vs ABSORPTION COSTING
Key Difference:
Fixed MOH: Absorption = Product Cost | Variable = Period Cost
Unit Product Cost:
Absorption: DM + DL + Var MOH + Fixed MOH/unit
Variable: DM + DL + Var MOH only
Income Statement:
Absorption: Sales - COGS = Gross Margin - S&A = NOI
Variable: Sales - Var COGS - Var S&A = CM - Fixed = NOI
When Income Differs:
Prod = Sales: Same income
Prod > Sales: Absorption > Variable (Fixed MOH in inventory)
Prod < Sales: Variable > Absorption (Absorption expenses old Fixed MOH)
Difference = Δ Inventory × Fixed MOH/unit
BUDGETING (Ch 9)
Production Budget:
Units to Produce = Sales + Ending Inv - Beginning Inv
Materials Budget:
Materials Needed = Production × Materials/unit
Purchases = Needed + Ending Mat - Beginning Mat
Labor Budget:
Hours = Production × Hours/unit
Cost = Hours × Rate/hour
Cash Budget:
Ending Cash = Beginning + Receipts - Disbursements
Timing matters! Credit sales collected later, credit purchases paid later
Budget Flow:
Sales → Production → Materials/Labor/OH → Cash → Statements
VARIANCES (Ch 10)
Materials:
Price Var = AQ × (AP - SP)
Quantity Var = SP × (AQ - SQ)
Total = Price + Quantity
Labor:
Rate Var = AH × (AR - SR)
Efficiency Var = SR × (AH - SH)
Total = Rate + Efficiency
Variable OH:
Spending Var = Actual VOH - (AH × VOHR)
Efficiency Var = VOHR × (AH - SH)
Fixed OH:
Budget Var = Actual FOH - Budgeted FOH
Volume Var = Budgeted FOH - Applied FOH
Volume = Capacity utilization, not spending
Key:
SQ = Units PRODUCED × Standard/unit (not sold!)
F = Actual < Standard | U = Actual > Standard
RELEVANT COSTS (Ch 12)
Relevant = Future + Different + Avoidable
Sunk Costs = Always Irrelevant
Opportunity Cost = Always Relevant
Make-or-Buy:
Compare: Make (Var + Avoidable Fixed) vs Buy (Price)
Special Order:
Accept if: Price > Variable Costs (if capacity available)
Ignore fixed costs if capacity available
Product Mix:
Rank by: CM per Constrained Resource
Not CM per unit! Use constraint (hours, materials, etc.)
Keep or Drop:
Segment Margin = Revenue - Var - Traceable Fixed
Ignore allocated common fixed costs
PERFORMANCE MEASUREMENT (Ch 13)
ROI:
ROI = Net Operating Income ÷ Avg Operating Assets
ROI = Margin × Turnover
Margin = NOI ÷ Sales | Turnover = Sales ÷ Assets
Residual Income:
RI = NOI - (Assets × Required ROI)
RI encourages all investments above required return
ROI vs RI:
ROI may discourage good investments (if ROI < current)
RI encourages all profitable investments
Transfer Pricing:
Min = Variable Cost + Opportunity Cost
Max = External purchase price

1. Financial (ROI, Profit, Growth)

Cause-Effect Chain:
Learning → Processes → Customer → Financial
Leading indicators → Lagging indicators
PRE-MIDTERM ESSENTIALS
CVP Analysis:
CM/unit = Price - Var Cost | CM Ratio = CM ÷ Sales
Break-Even Units = Fixed Costs ÷ CM/unit
Target Profit = (Fixed + Profit) ÷ CM/unit
Margin of Safety = Actual - Break-Even

Process Costing:
